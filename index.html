Dr. Israr Ahmed

Israr Ahmed (1932–2010) was a distinguished Islamic scholar and revivalist from Pakistan.

He devoted his life to spreading the message of the Qur’an and advocating for Islamic reform and unity.

  • Born: April 26, 1932, in Hisar, British India.
  • Migration: Migrated to Pakistan with his family after the Partition in 1947 and settled in Lahore.
  • Education: Graduated from King Edward Medical College, Lahore (MBBS) in 1954.
  • Early Islamic Work: Became associated with the Islamic revival movement and joined Jamaat-e-Islami under the leadership of Maulana Abul Ala Maududi.
  • Career Shift: Left medical practice to dedicate his life to Islamic scholarship, preaching, and revival of Islamic teachings.
  • Da'wah Work: Delivered thousands of lectures across Pakistan and abroad, spreading the message of the Qur’an and Islamic revival.
  • Legacy: His teachings continue to inspire millions of Muslims around the world through his books, recorded lectures, and the institutions he established.
  • Qur’anic Studies: Devoted decades to teaching and explaining the Qur’an, delivering lectures across Pakistan and internationally.
  • Founded: Established Markazi Anjuman Khuddam-ul-Qur’an in 1972 to promote understanding of the Qur’an.
  • Founded: Established Tanzeem-e-Islami in 1975, aiming for the revival of Islamic values and the establishment of an Islamic system.
  • Educational Efforts: Founded Qur’an Academy Lahore to spread Qur’anic education and research.
  • Author: Wrote several influential books and delivered thousands of lectures on Qur’anic philosophy, Islamic revival, and political thought.
  • Known For: Powerful Qur’anic lectures, deep analysis of modern challenges faced by the Muslim world, and advocacy for Islamic reform and Khilafah.
  • Recognition: Widely respected as one of the most influential Islamic scholars and speakers in Pakistan.
  • Died: April 14, 2010, in Lahore, Pakistan.
